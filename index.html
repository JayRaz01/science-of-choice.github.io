Fundamentals

This section covers the basic concepts and foundations of mathematical economics.

We explore what it means and takes to build a mathematical model in the world of decision theory (math-econ). This includes understanding the key principles, assumptions, and applications of mathematical models in economics.
This section covers the concepts of utility and preference, which are central to understanding how individuals make choices. We examine how utility functions are constructed and how preferences are represented and analyzed in economic models.

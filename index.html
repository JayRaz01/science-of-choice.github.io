Fundamentals

This section covers the basic concepts and foundations of mathematical economics.

We explore what it means and takes to build a mathematical model in the world of decision theory (math-econ). This includes understanding the key principles, assumptions, and applications of mathematical models in economics.
This section covers the concepts of utility and preference, which are central to understanding how individuals make choices. We examine how utility functions are constructed and how preferences are represented and analyzed in economic models.
An introduction to the basic concepts of game theory, including the formulation of games, strategies, and the concept of Nash equilibrium. We also discuss the relevance of game theory in economic decision-making.

Intermediate Topics

Dive deeper into more complex theories and applications within mathematical economics.

Building upon basic game theory, this section explores more advanced concepts such as mixed strategies, repeated games, and evolutionary game theory, emphasizing strategic interaction in more complex settings.
This section examines decision-making when there are no constraints, exploring how individuals optimize their choices in a variety of economic settings.
Explore how constraints, such as budget limits or time restrictions, affect decision-making, and how individuals optimize their choices under such constraints.
Delve into the theory of decision-making when outcomes are uncertain, including expected utility theory, risk aversion, and how individuals manage uncertainty in economic models.
This section introduces dynamic models in economics, focusing on how decisions evolve over time, including basic dynamic programming and its applications in economic contexts.

Advanced Topics

Explore advanced and cutting-edge topics in mathematical economics.

This section delves into advanced concepts in game theory, such as cooperative games, signaling, and repeated games with incomplete information. The focus is on strategic decision-making in complex environments.
Explore how decision-making processes evolve over time when there are no constraints. This includes dynamic optimization and intertemporal choice models.
This section examines how constraints influence decision-making over time, focusing on dynamic models where individuals optimize their choices under various temporal constraints.
This section explores how individuals make decisions over time when faced with uncertainty. Topics include stochastic dynamic programming and decision rules under uncertainty.
This section covers advanced topics in dynamic systems, including chaotic dynamics, non-linear models, and their applications in economic theory.
